# MesaFlow — Business Model

## Business Model Summary

MesaFlow is a B2B SaaS product sold directly to restaurants on a monthly subscription.

The initial model is simple:

- 30-day free pilot.
- No long-term contract.
- Paid subscription after the pilot.
- Initial target price: 29€/month per restaurant.
- Future possibility of tiered plans by restaurant size or message volume.

## Initial Commercial Offer

### Pilot Offer

**30 days free, no commitment.**

The pilot includes:

- QR code setup.
- Basic onboarding.
- Receptionist workflow explanation.
- Customer self-entry into waitlist.
- Confirmation message.
- Staff call button.
- Customer notification when called.
- Feedback collection.

### Paid Offer

**MesaFlow Starter — 29€/month per restaurant**

Includes:

- QR-based digital waitlist.
- Customer self-entry.
- Reception/front-of-house panel.
- Customer queue position.
- Immediate confirmation after joining the queue.
- “Call Customer” action.
- WhatsApp notification when called, preferably automatic.
- Manual customer entry as fallback.
- No permanence contract.

## Pricing Logic

29€/month is intentionally accessible for Portuguese small and medium restaurants.

The pricing must feel:

- Low-risk.
- Easy to approve.
- Cheaper than the operational pain of losing customers.
- Cheaper than staff confusion during peak service.
- Simple enough not to require procurement or long negotiation.

## Future Pricing Options

Future pricing may evolve into:

### Option A — Simple Tiering

- Starter: small restaurants.
- Pro: high-volume restaurants.
- Multi-location: small chains.

### Option B — Base + Message Usage

- Fixed monthly base.
- Included message allowance.
- Extra SMS/WhatsApp cost above allowance.

### Option C — Volume-Based Plans

- Based on number of customers joined/called per month.

## Recommended Initial Pricing Strategy

Start with one simple paid plan at 29€/month after the pilot.

Do not introduce complex pricing before validating willingness to pay.

Message costs should be monitored. If WhatsApp/SMS costs become meaningful, introduce message limits or transparent overage pricing later.

## Revenue Goal

12-month target:

- 50 paying restaurants.
- At 29€/month: 1,450€ MRR.

This is not yet a venture-scale revenue target. It is a validation target proving demand, retention and sales motion.

## 90-Day Goal

The first 90-day commercial goal is:

- 5 paying restaurants.

This is the first proof that restaurants are willing to pay after trying the product.

## Sales Motion

Initial sales should be founder-led and presencial.

Primary territory:

- Barreiro.
- Setúbal.
- Seixal.

Initial approach:

1. Identify restaurants with visible queues or dinner peaks.
2. Visit personally outside peak hours.
3. Pitch a 30-day free pilot.
4. Offer simple setup and QR code.
5. Collect feedback.
6. Convert to paid after pilot.

Future acquisition channels may include:

- Cold email.
- Direct mail.
- Local partnerships.
- Referrals from restaurant owners.
- Case studies.
- Simple website and inbound content.

## Conversion Requirements

A restaurant should pay after the pilot if:

- The team used MesaFlow without extensive training.
- It reduced confusion during peak hours.
- The receptionist prefers it to paper.
- Customers understood the flow.
- The restaurant looked more professional.
- The owner/manager sees value in keeping it.

## Key Business Risks

1. Restaurants may not want to pay another monthly subscription.
2. Staff may ignore the system and return to paper.
3. Customers may resist QR entry.
4. WhatsApp/SMS costs may affect margins.
5. The product may appear too simple to charge for.

## Risk Mitigation

- Founder-led onboarding.
- Make usage easier than paper.
- Keep pricing low and clear.
- Avoid long contracts.
- Offer pilot first.
- Collect case studies showing reduced chaos and better customer flow.
