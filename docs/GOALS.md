# MesaFlow — Goals

## 90-Day Goals

### Primary Goal

Convert 5 restaurants into paying customers.

### Supporting Goals

- Run pilots with restaurants in Barreiro, Setúbal and Seixal.
- Complete at least 5 active 30-day pilots.
- Collect receptionist feedback from every pilot.
- Obtain at least 2 usable testimonials.
- Produce at least 1 strong case study.
- Validate whether restaurants will pay after using MesaFlow.
- Validate whether staff actually use MesaFlow during peak service.

## 12-Month Goals

### Primary Goal

Reach 50 paying restaurants.

### Supporting Goals

- Prove retention across multiple months.
- Build a repeatable founder-led sales process.
- Create a simple restaurant onboarding playbook.
- Establish initial presence in the Margem Sul restaurant market.
- Collect multiple testimonials and case studies.
- Validate pricing at 29€/month or determine a better model.
- Validate WhatsApp notification economics.
- Identify whether reservations, table management or wait-time prediction should become the next expansion.

## Product Goals

The product must allow restaurants to:

- Display a QR code for walk-in customers.
- Let customers join the waitlist themselves.
- Show the customer confirmation that they are on the list.
- Show their position in the queue.
- Let staff view current waiting customers.
- Let staff manually add customers as fallback.
- Let staff call a customer with one clear action.
- Send a WhatsApp notification when a customer is called.
- Avoid paper during peak service.

## Business Goals

MesaFlow must prove:

- Restaurants recognize the problem.
- Owners/managers are willing to try the solution.
- Receptionists can use the system without heavy training.
- The product is valuable enough to pay for.
- The sales cycle can be short and founder-led.
- The market is large enough to expand beyond the initial territory.

## Customer Success Goals

A successful restaurant account should show:

- Weekly usage.
- Waitlists managed without paper.
- Receptionist acceptance.
- Owner/manager perceived value.
- Reduced front-door confusion.
- Customers understanding the QR flow.

## What Not To Optimize Too Early

Do not optimize early for:

- Advanced analytics.
- Reservations.
- POS integrations.
- Complex table maps.
- Marketplace traffic.
- Customer loyalty.
- Multi-country expansion.
- Large enterprise accounts.

The first goal is not feature breadth.

The first goal is repeated usage and payment by real restaurants.
