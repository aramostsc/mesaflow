# MesaFlow — CEO Brain

## Strategic Thesis

MesaFlow should begin as a focused SaaS product that digitizes the restaurant waitlist using a QR code and a simple staff call workflow.

The opportunity exists because many restaurants still manage walk-in customers with paper and verbal communication, especially during dinner peaks. This creates stress, customer uncertainty and potential lost business.

The best first market is not all restaurants. The best first market is busy restaurants in the Margem Sul with visible walk-in pressure.

## Founder-Level Belief

The product must be so simple that the receptionist under pressure prefers MesaFlow to paper.

If paper feels faster, MesaFlow loses.

## Initial Customer Profile

Ideal first customers:

- Popular restaurants with queues.
- Tourist restaurants.
- Marisqueiras.
- Tascas with dinner rush.
- Grelhados/restaurants with high walk-in volume.
- Located in Barreiro, Setúbal and Seixal.
- Currently using paper or informal queue management.

## Buyer Psychology

The owner/manager will buy if they believe MesaFlow helps with:

- Losing fewer customers at the door.
- Reducing stress for staff.
- Making the restaurant look more professional.
- Organizing peak-hour flow.
- Avoiding complex software.

## User Psychology

The receptionist/front-of-house user will adopt if:

- It is fast.
- It is obvious.
- It does not create extra work.
- It does not fail during peak hours.
- It is easier to trust than paper.

## Customer Psychology

The waiting customer will use it if:

- No app install is required.
- The QR flow is simple.
- They receive immediate confirmation.
- They can see their position.
- They are notified clearly when called.

## Strategic Wedge

The wedge is not reservations.

The wedge is:

> Walk-in customers join the queue themselves by QR code.

This reduces staff work and creates a modern waiting experience.

## MVP Scope

Build only what is necessary for:

1. Customer scans QR.
2. Customer enters name, phone and party size.
3. Customer receives confirmation.
4. Customer sees queue position.
5. Staff sees waiting list.
6. Staff can manually add customer if needed.
7. Staff clicks “Call Customer”.
8. Customer receives WhatsApp notification.

## Explicit Non-Goals

MesaFlow is not initially:

- Reservation software.
- Table planning software.
- POS software.
- Marketplace.
- Review platform.
- Loyalty tool.
- Marketing automation tool.
- CRM.

## Pricing Thesis

Start simple:

- 30-day free pilot.
- 29€/month after pilot.
- No permanence.
- Founder-led setup.

Pricing can evolve after real usage and messaging costs are known.

## Sales Thesis

The first 50 customers will likely come from direct trust-building, not scalable marketing.

Start with presencial selling.

Use:

- Door-to-door restaurant visits.
- Short pitch.
- Free pilot.
- QR setup.
- Case studies.
- Referrals.

## Main Risks

1. Restaurants do not want another subscription.
2. Staff revert to old habits.
3. Customers do not use QR.
4. Messaging costs reduce margin.
5. Product feels too simple to pay for.

## CEO Decisions Already Made

- Start with waitlist, not full reservations.
- Use QR customer self-entry as core flow.
- Include manual staff entry as fallback.
- Include WhatsApp notification in MVP.
- Do not include wait-time prediction at launch.
- Do not become a marketplace.
- Do not promise guaranteed revenue increase.
- Target 5 paying restaurants in 90 days.
- Target 50 paying restaurants in 12 months.

## CEO Open Questions

Still to validate through pilots:

- Will restaurants pay 29€/month after trial?
- Will staff use MesaFlow during peak pressure?
- Will customers reliably scan QR codes?
- Is WhatsApp notification economically viable at this price?
- Are restaurants more interested in “less stress” or “fewer lost customers” messaging?
- Which segment converts best: marisqueiras, tascas, tourist restaurants or grelhados?

## CEO Rule

Do not expand the product until the waitlist loop is retained and paid for.
