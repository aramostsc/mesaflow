# MesaFlow — Positioning

## Positioning Statement

MesaFlow is the simple QR waitlist system for busy restaurants that want less chaos at the door, fewer lost customers and a more professional waiting experience without becoming a marketplace or complex reservation platform.

## Core Promise

> Less chaos at the door. More tables occupied.

Secondary promise:

> Transform your restaurant queue into a simple and professional experience.

## Category

MesaFlow should initially define itself as:

**Digital waitlist software for restaurants.**

Not:

- Restaurant marketplace.
- Full reservation system.
- POS software.
- CRM.
- Marketing automation platform.
- Full restaurant management suite.

## Target Customer

Initial target:

- Busy restaurants in Barreiro, Setúbal and Seixal.
- Tourist restaurants.
- Popular seafood restaurants and tascas.
- Grilled-food restaurants with dinner peaks.
- Restaurants where walk-ins appear without reservation.
- Restaurants that currently use paper/caderno for waiting lists.

## Buyer

The buyer is normally:

- Owner.
- Manager.

They care about:

- Not losing customers.
- Reducing team stress.
- Looking more professional.
- Keeping operations simple.
- Avoiding complex software.

## Daily User

The daily user is primarily:

- Receptionist.
- Front-of-house staff member.

They care about:

- Speed.
- Few clicks.
- Not losing names.
- Calling customers easily.
- Having a list they can trust during peak service.

## End Customer

The waiting customer cares about:

- Knowing they are on the list.
- Knowing their queue position.
- Being notified clearly when called.
- Not needing to download an app.
- Not feeling forgotten.

## Main Alternatives

Current alternatives include:

- Paper/caderno.
- Verbal waiting list.
- WhatsApp improvisation.
- Phone calls.
- Memory of the receptionist.
- Full reservation platforms.
- POS/table systems with more complexity.

## Differentiation

MesaFlow wins by being:

- Simpler.
- Faster during service.
- Focused on walk-ins and waitlists.
- Built for small and medium restaurants.
- Not a marketplace.
- Not commission-based.
- No customer app download.
- Restaurante-friendly, not “tech-heavy”.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Operational Efficiency

Organize the queue without paper or confusion.

### 2. Less Stress

Give reception staff a clear list and simple call action.

### 3. Better Customer Experience

Customers enter the queue themselves and receive confirmation.

### 4. Professional First Impression

The restaurant entrance feels modern and organized.

### 5. Simplicity

No complicated setup. No long training. No marketplace.

## Tagline Options

Primary:

> Menos caos à porta. Mais mesas ocupadas.

Alternative:

> A fila digital simples para restaurantes movimentados.

Alternative:

> Organize a espera. Chame clientes. Esqueça o papel.

## Positioning Decision

MesaFlow should be positioned as an operational tool, not a marketing marketplace.

The product should sell efficiency, calm and professionalism first.

Revenue increase can be implied as a possible result, but must not be guaranteed.
