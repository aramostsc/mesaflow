# MesaFlow — Vision

## Long-Term Vision

Build the simplest and most intuitive restaurant waitlist and front-of-house flow software in Portugal, with the option to expand into a broader European SaaS product for small and medium restaurants.

MesaFlow exists to make the restaurant entrance calmer, faster and more professional.

## Product Vision

MesaFlow should become the default lightweight tool for restaurants that still manage walk-ins with paper, memory, shouting, WhatsApp improvisation or informal queues.

The product should help restaurants:

- Organize walk-in demand.
- Reduce front-door confusion.
- Reduce stress for the reception/front-of-house team.
- Give waiting customers clarity.
- Avoid losing customers who leave because the wait feels unmanaged.
- Modernize the entrance experience without becoming complex.

## Strategic Direction

MesaFlow should not start as a full restaurant management platform.

The first strategic wedge is:

> A QR-based digital waitlist for busy restaurants where customers join the queue themselves and the restaurant calls them when the table is ready.

Future expansion may include reservations, table management and wait-time prediction, but only after the waitlist product is validated and retained by paying restaurants.

## Company Ambition

MesaFlow begins as a small, profitable SaaS opportunity with the potential to become a national Portuguese restaurant SaaS and, eventually, a sellable product or acquisition target.

The 12-month ambition is:

- 50 paying restaurants.
- Strong retention.
- Clear case studies.
- Simple product usage during real service hours.
- Proof that restaurants are willing to pay for digital waitlist management.

## What MesaFlow Must Never Become

MesaFlow must never become:

- A marketplace like TheFork.
- A complex all-in-one restaurant system too early.
- A software product full of menus and configuration.
- A product that requires customers to install an app.
- A product that creates more work than paper.
- A product that promises guaranteed revenue increases.

## Core Strategic Belief

Restaurants do not need more software complexity.

They need fewer moments of chaos.

MesaFlow wins if it becomes easier than paper.
