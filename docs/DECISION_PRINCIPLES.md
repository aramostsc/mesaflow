# MesaFlow — Decision Principles

## Core Principle

Every decision must make restaurant queue management simpler, faster or more reliable.

If a feature does not help during real service pressure, it should probably not be built yet.

## Decision Questions

Before approving any product, business or growth decision, ask:

1. Does this solve a real restaurant problem?
2. Is this problem frequent enough?
3. Does this reduce work for the restaurant?
4. Does this make the customer experience clearer?
5. Is it simple enough for a receptionist during peak service?
6. Does it avoid unnecessary setup or training?
7. Can it scale beyond the first restaurant?
8. Does it support the business model?
9. Does it help conversion, retention or strong case studies?
10. Does it keep MesaFlow focused?

## Prioritization Order

1. Critical customer problems.
2. Critical bugs.
3. UX improvements that reduce friction.
4. Performance and reliability.
5. Strategic features.
6. Optional features.

## Product Philosophy

Favor:

- Fewer features.
- Higher quality.
- More speed.
- Clearer workflows.
- Less training.
- More consistency.

Avoid:

- Menus inside menus.
- Configuration-heavy workflows.
- Features used rarely.
- Complex onboarding.
- Enterprise-style software feel.
- Overpromising business results.

## MVP Feature Acceptance Rules

A feature may enter the MVP only if it helps one of these flows:

- Customer joins the queue.
- Customer receives confirmation.
- Staff sees the waitlist.
- Staff calls the customer.
- Customer receives the call notification.
- Staff can add a customer manually when needed.

Everything else is suspect until validated.

## Features To Refuse Initially

Refuse or postpone:

- Full reservation system.
- Visual table map.
- Online payments.
- Digital menus.
- Reviews.
- Loyalty program.
- CRM.
- Marketing campaigns.
- POS integration.
- Advanced analytics.
- Marketplace features.

## Commercial Decision Rules

Pricing and packaging must be:

- Easy to understand.
- Low-risk for restaurants.
- Simple to sell in person.
- Compatible with message costs.
- Flexible enough to evolve after pilots.

Do not introduce complex pricing before validating payment behavior.

## Customer Experience Rules

The end customer must:

- Not install an app.
- Understand what to do immediately.
- Get confirmation after joining.
- See their place in the queue.
- Be notified clearly when called.

## Operational Rules

For restaurant staff, MesaFlow must be:

- Faster than paper or close to it.
- More reliable than memory.
- Easy under pressure.
- Trustworthy during peak hours.
- Useful without extensive training.

## Strategic Rule

MesaFlow wins by being focused.

Do not become a full restaurant operating system before winning the queue.
